Women's Programs

Women's Wellness & Inspiration

 

More than 200 women from across the community came together for an uplifting Evening of Women's Wellness & Inspiration. The event was co-hosted by Dr. Ariella Altman, Surgical Oncologist at Allina Health, who highlighted the importance of women's health and taking an active role in wellness. The key feature of the evening was our guest speaker Charlene Aminoff, who inspired the audience with her heartfelt message about choosing faith over fear, discovering joy even through life's challenges, and recognizing the strength that comes from trusting in G-d through every season. It was an evening of connection, empowerment, and inspiration that left women feeling uplifted and renewed.

Holidays

Lag B'Omer

 

Lag B'Omer is known throughout the world as a day of celebration and Jewish unity. Here in Minneapolis we celebrated with an outdoor event on the front lawn of the Chabad Center, with a picnic and great activities. Remote control cars and a petting zoo were some of the highlights of the evening. Above all, the community coming together to celebrate is truly a cherished moment.

All Night Learning

Shavuot celebrations began with a Yom Tov meal accompanying a learning session led by Rabbi Mordechai & Rivkie Grossbaum. The learning continued with a class led by Rabbi Shmuly into the early hours of the morning.

Around the Community

Hebrew School Graduation

 

The end-of-year celebration honored our students, who have grown so remarkably this past year: the Alef Champs Hebrew reading program, and the Jew-Q Jewish Knowledge competition, were some of our remarkable teaching tools. Above all, the confidence of knowing who they are as Jews, living in our American society, is truly noteworthy. Graduates took the stage before proud parents and grandparents to receive their diplomas - and to serve as an inspiration as the next generation.

A Life of Leadership

Gimmel Tammuz

One People, One Heart

 

Marking the anniversary of the Rebbe's passing, the Twin Cities community gathered for an evening devoted to his life, his leadership, and the mission he entrusted to each of us. Insights of his Torah teachings and lessons gleaned from stories of his life made it a meaningful and uplifting event.

The evening began with a musical presentation by the Mecham Duo - uniquely talented violinists playing Chassidic melodies. Rabbi Shalom Moshe Paltiel, director of Chabad of Port Washington, inspired the crowd with heartfelt stories and an uplifting message.

32 years after his passing, the Rebbe continues to be the address for blessing, guidance, and inspiration. A visit to the Rebbe's Ohel will leave you inspired and moved to act, as it does for tens of thousands of visitors of all backgrounds.

Two Weeks in the Kitchen

Kosher Culinary Camp

Cooking up a Storm!

 

Welcome to the Kosher Culinary Camp. What started in Lunds & Byerlys with 25 kids has, Thank G-D, grown to 206 kids and 25+ staff members - outgrowing even our new facility!

206

Campers

 25+

Staff Members

 2

Full Weeks

For two full weeks this summer, the Chabad Center belonged to the kids. Every day the campers created a new dish, learning and practicing new cooking skills as they made their way through a genuine hands-on culinary education - and along the way they absorbed the beauty of keeping kosher as something joyful and delicious.

Daily outings took the campers beyond the kitchen, with the opportunity to show their great Jewish pride! And no less important, over the course of two weeks, great Jewish friendships were formed!
Spotlight

Repeat. Success!

Some of the proudest faces at Kosher Culinary Camp this year belonged to a special group of staff: girls who attended camp as campers and returned this summer to the other side of the counter - planning and teaching the next generation of proud Jews through the summer of a lifetime. Watching them lead the very program they once attended is everything we hope for as a community.
